How to Compress a Video for Email (Under 25 MB)

Updated July 2026 · 7 min read · By VidZip Team

Email is still the hardest place to send a video. Gmail, Outlook, and iCloud Mail all cap attachments at roughly 25 MB — and almost any iPhone clip from the last few years sails past that in seconds. This guide shows exactly how to compress a video to fit under the email limit, with the bitrate math explained and the fastest one-tap method on iPhone.

The 25 MB Email Limit, Explained

Why 25 MB specifically? Email is an old protocol, and attachments get encoded into text (base64) for transit, which inflates their size by about 33%. To keep messages deliverable, most providers cap the encoded size — which works out to around 25 MB for the original file.

  • Gmail: 25 MB attachment limit
  • Outlook / Hotmail: 25 MB
  • iCloud Mail: ~20 MB
  • Yahoo Mail: 25 MB

Go over and your email client will refuse to send, silently drop the attachment, or pester you to upload to a cloud drive instead. To send an actual attachment, the file has to fit — and that means compressing.

How Long a Video Fits in 25 MB?

The math is simple: file size ≈ bitrate × duration. For a 25 MB target:

Video Duration Bitrate to fit 25 MB Likely Quality
30 seconds~6.7 MbpsExcellent (near original)
1 minute~3.3 MbpsVery good
3 minutes~1.1 MbpsGood on phone screen
5 minutes~0.7 MbpsAcceptable, soft on motion
10 minutes~0.33 MbpsNoticeably degraded

For typical clips under 2 minutes, 25 MB is plenty and quality stays high. For anything over 5 minutes, you'll see clear quality loss at this size — better to send a cloud link instead.

When Email Just Won't Work

If your video is too long for 25 MB to look acceptable (say, 10+ minutes of footage), email isn't the right tool. Options:

  • Google Drive link in Gmail. Gmail will prompt this automatically when a file exceeds 25 MB — the recipient clicks and streams/downloads.
  • iCloud link. In Photos, share → Options → "iCloud Link." Recipient opens in a browser.
  • WeTransfer. Free for up to 2 GB, sends a download link by email.
  • AirDrop if both devices are nearby — no size limit, no email needed.

But for typical short clips — a kid's birthday moment, a quick demo, a 30-second scene — compression to 25 MB works perfectly and is much less friction than a cloud link.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the email attachment size limit for video?

Gmail and Outlook both cap attachments at 25 MB. iCloud Mail is around 20 MB. Yahoo Mail allows up to 25 MB. To send a video by email, it must be under your provider's limit — most iPhone videos need to be compressed first.

How do I compress a video to 25 MB for email?

Use VidZip's Target Size mode: enter 25 MB and tap Compress. The app calculates the bitrate needed to hit exactly that size based on the video's duration. The result fits under Gmail and Outlook's attachment limit.

What if my video is still too large after compressing to 25 MB?

If 25 MB still doesn't fit (rare, but possible with very long videos), try 20 MB or lower. Alternatively, upload the video to Google Drive or iCloud and share a link in the email — that bypasses the attachment limit entirely.

How long of a video fits in 25 MB?

Roughly 3-5 minutes at phone-viewing quality (about 0.7-1.1 Mbps). Shorter if the footage has lots of motion. For typical 30-second to 2-minute clips, 25 MB is plenty and quality stays high.

Does Gmail automatically compress video attachments?

No. Gmail does not compress video attachments. If a video exceeds 25 MB, Gmail will refuse to attach it and instead prompt you to upload it to Google Drive and insert a Drive link. To send an actual attachment, you must compress the video yourself first.
